Audio samples of "A Contextual Tone Labeling Method of Neural Text-to-Speech for Tone Sandhi Languages"

Authors: Pengfei Shao, Minchuan Chen, Ting Wang, Xifan Zhang, Jiaying Xu, Jun Ma, Shaojun Wang, Jing Xiao

Neural network-based speech synthesis has made significant progress in recent years. Although it outperforms classical pipeline systems with high naturalness in English and Mandarin Chinese, it's applicability to other tonal languages is less explored. Shanghainese could be one of the most difficult languages to achieve end-to-end speech synthesis, largely due to its complex tone sandhi phenomenon.

Subjective evaluations

The following three speech synthesis systems are established for subjective preference tests in this paper.

  • Baseline system: None tone sandhi was used. We give each syllable their citation tone for training. So there are only 5 citation tone categories, 53, 34, 23, 55, 12.
  • Tra-SH system: Traditional tone sandhi rules shown in Table 1 of this paper are used. There are 10 tone categories, 53, 34, 23, 55, 12, 21, 33, 44, 11, 22.
  • Pro-SH system: Tone sandhi rules shown in Table 2 of this paper are used in our proposed system. There are 13 tone categories, 53, 34, 23, 50, 12, 55, 42, 21, 33, 44, 21, 11, 22.
